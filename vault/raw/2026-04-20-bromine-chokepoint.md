# The Bromine Chokepoint (Raw)

Source: https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/the-bromine-chokepoint-how-strife-in-the-middle-east-could-halt-production-of-the-worlds-memory-chips/
Fetched: 2026-04-20

## Summary

Bromine is a raw material converted into semiconductor-grade hydrogen bromide gas, used as an etch chemical in manufacturing DRAM and NAND flash memory chips.

## Why Bromine Matters
- Hydrogen bromide achieves polysilicon-to-oxide selectivity ratio of 100:1 vs chlorine's 30:1
- Essential for etching transistor structures at advanced chip geometries
- No viable alternative exists

## Geopolitical Risk
- South Korea sources 97.5% of bromine imports from Israel
- Israel's ICL Group operates extraction at a single Dead Sea facility
- Negev region has already endured ballistic missile attacks within 35km of ICL's complex
- No other conversion facilities exist outside Israel capable of immediate scale-up

## Key Statistics
- Samsung + SK hynix: ~70% of global DRAM market
- SK hynix: ~57% of high-bandwidth memory market
- DRAM suppliers maintain only 2-3 weeks inventory
- War risk insurance for Israeli ports: 0.2% → 0.7-1.0% per voyage
- Smartphone prices in Bangladesh rose 10-25% in 2026 due to memory inflation
