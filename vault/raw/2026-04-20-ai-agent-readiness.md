# AIエージェント元年はいつ来るのか (Raw)

Source: https://wired.jp/article/sz-ai-agents-math-doesnt-add-up/
Fetched: 2026-04-20

## Key Arguments

### Skeptical View
Research paper claims LLMs have mathematically inherent limitations preventing reliable agent execution beyond simple word prediction, making them unsuitable for critical tasks.

### Optimistic Counterargument
Industry leaders cite real breakthroughs in coding applications and mathematical verification methods (e.g., Lean programming language) that could overcome hallucination issues through guardrails.

## Why Agents Haven't Taken Off
- Hallucinations remain persistent: "精度が100%に達することは決してない" (achieving 100% accuracy is impossible)
- Unreliability prevents full enterprise adoption
- Handling hallucinations disrupts workflows

## What's Needed
- Guardrails and verification systems
- Recognition that hallucinations are inherent features, not fixable bugs
- Human verification layers always required
- Incremental improvement focus rather than transformative breakthroughs

## Conclusion
Rather than a single "agent year," expect yearly incremental progress. The real question: how will large-scale cognitive automation reshape work and society?
