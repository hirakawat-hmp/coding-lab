# California Driverless Car Ticketing Law (2026-05-03)

Source: https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clypjx3rg2go
Also: https://sfist.com/2026/04/29/authorities-can-fine-driverless-cars-deploy-emergency-geofencing-starting-july-1/

Effective July 1, 2026: California DMV issues "Notices of AV Noncompliance" when driverless cars break traffic laws.

New powers:
- Police can ticket manufacturers directly for traffic violations
- Authorities can stipulate fleet sizes
- Geofencing deployment in emergencies
- Remote operators must be licensed and permitted

Reporting: incidents within 72 hours (24 hours for serious cases).

Penalties: DMV can restrict fleet size, speed, geography, and limit operations in bad weather.

Also: new regulations allow driverless trucks on California roadways.

Background: Previously driverless cars had been immune to traffic citations. Waymo incident (luggage left in trunk) highlighted accountability gap.
