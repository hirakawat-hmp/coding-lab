## Summary

SF author Martha Wells appeared as guest of honor at "Harcon" (はるこん) in Kawasaki, April 2026. Discussed Murderbot Diaries, neurodiversity, and physical AI.

## Key Insights

- Murderbot's first-person voice emerged naturally during writing
- Wells discovered her own autism spectrum traits while creating a character with social anxiety
- Murderbot narrative ends with AI choosing independence over "benevolent captivity" with humans
- Current LLMs engineered for neurotypical interaction patterns; Murderbot's authentic neurodivergent portrayal contrasts sharply
- Wells expressed concern her fiction might influence real AI research: "it's a little scary"
- Murderbot + ART (research vessel AI) relationship models distributed intelligence across environment and body
- Wells emphasizes danger of conflating non-conscious chatbots with genuinely conscious fictional AIs
