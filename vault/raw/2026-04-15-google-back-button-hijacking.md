# Raw: Google's New Spam Policy for Back Button Hijacking

Source: https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2026/04/back-button-hijacking
Fetched: 2026-04-15

## Summary

Google Search Central announced a new spam policy targeting "back button hijacking" — when websites manipulate browser navigation to prevent users from returning to their previous page.

## What Is Back Button Hijacking?

Back button hijacking occurs when websites manipulate browser navigation to prevent users from returning to their previous page. Instead of going back, users are redirected elsewhere — typically to spam, malware, or unwanted content.

## The New Policy

The policy treats back button hijacking as a violation of search quality standards, meaning affected websites may face ranking penalties or removal from search results.

## Why It Matters

- Degrades user experience: fundamental browser control violated
- Google enforcement signals commitment to protecting user trust
- Aligns with broader effort to combat deceptive UX practices
