## Key Claims

1. AES-128 and SHA-256 remain secure against quantum computers
2. Symmetric key sizes require no changes for post-quantum transition
3. Grover's Algorithm: theoretical quadratic speedup diminishes significantly when parallelized
4. Breaking AES-128 via Grover's: 140 trillion quantum circuits of 724 logical qubits each, 10 years
5. Cost: ~2^104.5 operations
6. Comparison: breaking AES-128 is ~430 sextillion times more expensive than breaking 256-bit EC via Shor's
7. Expert consensus: NIST, BSI, Samuel Jaques all agree 128-bit symmetric = no quantum risk
8. NIST quote: "AES 128 will remain secure for decades to come"
