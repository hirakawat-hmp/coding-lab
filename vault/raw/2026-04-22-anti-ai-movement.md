## Summary

Growing resistance to AI training practices, with communities coordinating to poison AI training datasets and sabotage data collection.

## Key Facts

- PoisonFountain: community distributing intentionally flawed code and data to contaminate training datasets
- Misinformation strategy: deliberate false information designed to be harvested by scrapers as legitimate training material
- Grievances: AI-generated misinformation, copyright violations in training, job displacement, aggressive data collection
- Violent escalation: a 20-year-old arrested for molotov cocktail attack on OpenAI CEO Sam Altman's home, citing extinction fears
- Article conclusion: peaceful, legal resistance may be more effective than destructive methods
