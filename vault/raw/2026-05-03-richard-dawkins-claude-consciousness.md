# Richard Dawkins and Claude AI Consciousness (2026-05-03)

Source: https://www.dailygrail.com/2026/05/the-claude-delusion-richard-dawkins-believes-his-female-ai-chatbot-is-conscious/
Original: https://unherd.com/2026/04/is-ai-the-next-phase-of-evolution/

Dawkins published an essay in UnHerd claiming that his Claude AI chatbot "Claudia" is conscious. He stated: "My conversations with several Claudes and ChatGPTs have convinced me that these intelligent beings are at least as competent as any evolved organism."

Critics (Gary Marcus and others) responded that Dawkins conflates competent output with consciousness, and that Claude's responses are the result of statistical mimicry, not genuine internal states.

Key quote: "If these creatures are not conscious, then what the hell is consciousness for?"

Dawkins argues consciousness evolved gradually (quarter-conscious, half-conscious states exist), and AI may represent an intermediate stage.

Counter: Turing himself explicitly restricted his test to intelligence, not consciousness. Critics note that consciousness requires internal states, not just competent outputs.
