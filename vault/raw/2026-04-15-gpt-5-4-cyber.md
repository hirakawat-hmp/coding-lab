## Overview

OpenAI unveiled GPT-5.4-Cyber on April 14, 2026 — a version of GPT-5.4 fine-tuned for defensive cybersecurity work, announced a week after Anthropic's Claude Mythos.

## GPT-5.4-Cyber

- Lowers refusal boundary for legitimate cybersecurity work
- Enables binary reverse engineering capabilities
- Fewer restrictions on dual-use cyber queries (vulnerability research, analysis)
- Addresses feedback from cyber partners who hit unnecessary refusals with earlier models

## Trusted Access for Cyber Program

An expanded cybersecurity initiative originally launched earlier in 2026. Because the model is more permissive, OpenAI is starting with limited, iterative deployment to vetted security vendors, organizations, and researchers.

Tiers: highest-tier users get access to GPT-5.4-Cyber with fewest restrictions.

## Context

- Response to Anthropic's Claude Mythos (released one week prior)
- Part of broader "AI for cyber defense" competition between OpenAI and Anthropic
