## Summary

Kaminashi engineer "Shimmy" argues that AI-accelerated coding should redirect focus from building more features to improving decision precision and customer delivery speed. Based on Paul Graham's "Do Things That Don't Scale" concept.

## Key Arguments

- Increased feature velocity creates "intent-diluted code" and maintenance debt in B2B
- Engineers should participate in customer visits, sales activities, and research
- Information infrastructure (Centou for research management) enables team-wide leverage
- Validation cycles should drive development schedules, not vice versa
- Engineer value increasingly exists outside code through cross-functional understanding
- Bottleneck is rarely in code; often in customer understanding or sales/delivery

## Core Insight

AI makes coding faster, but the bottleneck shifts upstream. The answer is not to build more features faster, but to participate directly in the non-scalable human work that improves product-market fit.
